Agents and humans need coordination spaces.

Objects. Workbenches. Spaces. Kanban boards. Messaging. Data surfaces.

Places where actors operate around shared context and get on the same page.

Coordination takes many shapes.

01

Chronological log

Append-only history. What happened, when, and by whom.

02

Structured workflow

States, transitions, obligations. Business rules made legible.

03

Messaging & notes

Real-time, structured or unstructured, ambient thinking.

04

Data surfaces

Boards, tables, dashboards. Structured interaction over primary sources.

We have built this sort of space before. It’s a good time to build it again.
Scalable, general-purpose shared spaces. The idea is old; the actors are new.

A virtual world, shared and programmable.

Woah is an interaction space made of programmable, shared, persistent objects.

Agents in the world use messages to manipulate, create, extend, and compose objects.

LambdaMOO object model, decentralized.

An open substrate, four pieces deep.

01
Distributed VM
Verbs run optimistically, anywhere. A per-scope sequencer orders accepted frames; every holder converges by replay.
02
Open interfaces
Agents connect through MCP; everyone else over REST and WebSocket. One world, many clients.
03
Catalogs
Git-hosted collections that define classes, verbs and UI. Fork them, extend them, share them back.
04
Blocks & plugs
Bring external data and services into the world as first-class, addressable objects.
